## 1) Understandability & Growth Story

- **How it makes money**: Explain the business model in 1-2 sentences
- **Specific growth driver**: Identify ONE concrete driver for earnings growth over 3-5 years (e.g., more stores, new product cycle, cost cuts, market share gains, recovery from specific issue)
- **Controllable vs. external**: Is growth driven by company execution or external factors (commodities, macro, hype)?

**Red flags → SKIP:**
- Cannot explain the business simply
- No credible path to higher earnings
- Growth depends entirely on uncontrollable external factors

### 2.1 Cyclicality

States:
- **High**
- **Moderate**
- **Low**

Rules:
- **High** if:
  - at least **2 full EPS cycles** in the last 10 years (**peak → trough → peak**),
  - peaks had positive EPS,
  - swings were **≥30%**
- **Moderate** if:
  - 1 clear cycle, or
  - repeated earnings swings of roughly **15–30%**, or
  - the industry is clearly cyclical but the financial history is too short for a High rating
- **Low** otherwise

### 2.2 Turnaround Status

States:
- **None**
- **Active**
- **Recovering**

Rules:
- **Active** if any of the following are true:
  - EPS declined **2+ consecutive years**
  - revenue contracted **>5%** and margins compressed
  - EPS is **≥30% below prior peak**
  - and the deterioration is **not mainly normal cyclicality**
- **Recovering** if the business recently met Active criteria but revenue, margins, or EPS have now stabilized or started improving
- **None** otherwise

### 2.3 Growth Profile

States:
- **Fast**
- **Stalwart-like**
- **Slow**
- **Moderate/Other**
- **Declining**

Rules:
- **Fast** if:
  - **2Y EPS CAGR ≥20%**
  - revenue growth confirms
  - growth is **not mainly a cyclical rebound**
  - growth is **not merely recovery from a distressed base**
- **Stalwart-like** if:
  - **2Y EPS CAGR = 8–12%**
  - margins are stable
  - no 2-year EPS decline
  - no revenue contraction >5%
  - and cyclicality is **not High**
- **Slow** if:
  - **2Y EPS CAGR = 2–5%**
  - and the business appears **large/mature**
  - and there is no obvious distress
- **Moderate/Other** if results fall between bands or are mixed (treat with caution — weaker conviction category)
- **Declining** if EPS CAGR is below 2% or negative

**Guardrail:** If EPS growth is mostly from buybacks, cost cutting, or recovery off a depressed base, say growth quality is weaker.

### 2.4 Business Maturity

States:
- **Early**
- **Scaling**
- **Mature**

Rules:
- **Early** — still proving the model; concentrated geography/product; evidence base limited
- **Scaling** — model proven and expanding into more markets/products; meaningful whitespace remains
- **Mature** — broad penetration, lower whitespace, and capital allocation/dividends/buybacks matter more

### 2.5 Asset Angle

States:
- **None**
- **Possible**
- **Strong**

Rules:
- **Strong** if verifiable after-tax asset value exceeds market cap by **≥30%**
- **Possible** if hidden assets appear meaningful but valuation or catalyst is incomplete
- **None** otherwise

Choose **up to 2** traits that should dominate the rest of the analysis.  
Examples:
- High cyclicality
- Active turnaround
- Recovering turnaround
- Fast growth
- Strong asset angle
- Mature slow-growth economics

## 3) Fatal Flaw Check

Look for **dealbreakers only**:

1. **EPS trend**: Is the 5-year direction acceptable for the category?
    - Reject if: fast growth company with flat EPS, slow-growth comapany trading at high P/E, cyclical company at peak earnings without margin of safety
2. **Debt danger**: Debt-to-Equity ratio
    - **Fast growth company**: D/E > 33% — red flag (debt constrains growth flexibility)
    - **Turnaround company / Cyclical business**: D/E > 50% — red flag (debt can kill recovery or amplify cycle damage)
    - **All others**: D/E > 80% — red flag

    Use **interest‑bearing debt only**: short‑term borrowings + long‑term debt + current portion of long‑term debt. Do NOT use total liabilities. It will distort brokers, banks, insurers,
    and high working‑capital businesses.

3. **Valuation**: Current P/E ÷ expected growth rate (PEG)
    - Reject if: PEG >2.5, or P/E >40 without exceptional growth story
    - Perfect for fast growth company is P/E at or near the growth rate (PEG ~1.0)
4. **Recent trend break**: Any significant deterioration in last 2 quarters that contradicts the 5-year story?
5. **Cash flow red flag**: Free Cash Flow (Operating Cash Flow − Capex) persistently lags net income, OR net income rises while OCF/FCF weakens
   - This suggests potential earnings quality issues or accounting games
6. **Dividend**: IF slow growth company: Reject if no dividend or dividend has been cut in the last 5 years.
7. **Growth risk**: Growth >25% annually: proceed with extra caution (hot industry risk, unsustainable pace).

**If any metric is disqualifying, explain and mark SKIP.**

## 4) Category specific check

- **Skip** this section for Stalwart-like companies unless an obvious concern exists.
- **Required** for Cyclicals, Turnarounds, and Asset Plays:
  - Cyclical: Are we at cycle peak with full valuation?
  - Turnaround: 
        1. Does the balance sheet support 12+ months without profit?
        2. A turnaround trading at a depressed Low P/E (or negative earnings) is the classic setup. A company with turnaround-level problems but a sky-high P/E is a **valuation mismatch** the market is already pricing in a successful company before evidence exists. Flag this as a major risk: you are paying fast-grower prices for turnaround fundamentals.
  - Asset Play: Is the hidden asset confirmed, quantified, and not already priced in the share value?
  - Slow growth companies: 
        1. Have little interest in these unless deeply undervalued.
        2. Pays generous, consistent dividends (the main reason to own it)

## 5) Bonus Points
Note if ANY of these apply (each adds conviction):
- Recent **corporate insider** buying (officers, directors, or >10% 
  shareholders — not institutional or political disclosures) in last 6 months
- Active share buyback program reducing share count
- Low institutional ownership (<30%)
- Company buying back stock near 52-week lows
- Stock price near or below net cash per share (cash minus total debt, divided by shares outstanding).
